It all starts to go downhill when Elliot gradually loses his self-control. He can go for days living as Mr. Robot, and when he snaps out of it, he no longer remembers anything he did. His successful plan-which is conducted in the end by Elliot or Mr. Robot?- has brought the world in an apocalyptic state and he is being chased not only by one of the strongest people in the world, the Chinese minister of State Security who happens to be the leader of a Chinese hacker group, but also by the FBI. This hacker group consists of extremely dangerous people who kill whoever stands in their way, like they end up doing with Angela and Darlene’s boyfriend.

Elliot and his father

It may look very fascinating witnessing what could possibly result by a single hacking act, even if it is expressed excessively. Everything falls apart in a chain react. Still, this is not the actual message this series is trying to send to us. It is much deeper, and it is very beautifully expressed by the perfect cinematography that always manages to catch the character’s emotions, in order to highlight what Elliot and the rest is really going through. Elliot’s struggle in isolation and his fight through depression. He starts to question reality. Is he who he thinks he is? Is anything really happening? In the end it becomes obvious that his Dissociative Identity Disorder goes from the childhood trauma he has experienced by his parents, especially by his father’s abuse.
